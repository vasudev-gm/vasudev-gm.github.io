### Quick Report

Razer CEO Min-Liang Tan voiced concerns about generative AI "slop" in gaming, stressing the need for quality and immersion. He advocates for AI as a tool to augment, not replace, human creativity, and highlights Razer\'s $600M investment in responsible AI development.
<!-- more -->

Tan discussed Project AVA, a holographic AI desk companion, and Razer\'s open, multi-model approach to AI hardware. The company aims to build trustworthy, user-controlled AI companions, focusing on creativity, transparency, and responsible experimentation. Reservations for AVA are open in the US, reflecting early interest in this emerging category.
