### Quick Report

AMD has expanded its Versal Prime Series Gen 2 portfolio with three new adaptive SoCs: 2VM3454, 2VM3254, and 2VM3104. The additions are designed for embedded markets that need strong compute density in smaller packages, with AMD citing up to 100k DMIPs and package sizes down to 23 mm x 23 mm.
<!-- more -->

Compared with higher-end eight-core variants, the new parts use a leaner processing system with four Cortex-A78AE application cores, six Cortex-R52 real-time cores, and a smaller Mali-G78AE GPU while still targeting up to 5x scalar compute versus prior AMD adaptive SoCs. AMD also emphasized common footprints and migration paths across multiple devices to reduce board redesign effort and speed time to market.
