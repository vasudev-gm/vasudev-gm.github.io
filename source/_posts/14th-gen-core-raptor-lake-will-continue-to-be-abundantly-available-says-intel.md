### Quick Report

Intel says it will keep 14th Gen Core **Raptor Lake Refresh** processors broadly available, alongside continued support for the LGA1700 ecosystem and 700-series motherboards. The company described Raptor Lake as still central to client strategy, signaling that mainstream desktop buyers should continue to see supply in channel through 2026.
<!-- more -->

Intel also highlighted the platform\'s dual-memory flexibility, with both DDR4 and DDR5 options still relevant amid elevated DDR5 pricing. Beyond consumer demand, the prolonged run helps Intel continue using its Intel 7 manufacturing capacity while newer desktop products increasingly rely on external foundry nodes for major silicon tiles.
