### Quick Report

SK hynix has unveiled 16 Gb LPDDR6 memory built on its sixth-generation 10 nm-class 1c process, with mass production expected in the first half of 2026 and customer availability targeted for the second half. The company says the new generation can exceed 10.7 Gbps and is being prepared for substantially higher speed bins in future implementations.
<!-- more -->

According to SK hynix, LPDDR6 also brings meaningful power-efficiency gains beyond LPDDR5X through architectural and control changes, including sub-channel behavior and DVFS-based scaling. That combination is positioned to improve peak throughput for gaming and AI workloads while reducing energy use in lighter mobile scenarios.
