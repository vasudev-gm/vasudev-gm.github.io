### Quick Report

Lexar says gamers building new PCs are more willing to scale down memory capacity than buy SSDs smaller than 512 GB, based on customer behavior discussed during a media tour interview with Digital Foundry. The takeaway is that storage floor expectations have hardened even as component pricing remains volatile.<!-- more -->

The company notes that buyers often see sub-512 GB SSDs as too restrictive for modern game libraries, where single titles can consume tens to hundreds of gigabytes after updates. In practice, this pushes many builds toward larger SSDs first, while memory capacity is adjusted to fit budget limits.

That shift matters because memory and NAND pricing have both been pressured by AI era demand, with recent market reports highlighting large DRAM swings over the past year. If these trends continue, mainstream gaming builds may keep favoring storage headroom over higher RAM tiers until pricing normalizes.
