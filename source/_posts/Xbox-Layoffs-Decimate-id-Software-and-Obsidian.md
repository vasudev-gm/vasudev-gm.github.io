### Quick Report

Microsoft\'s latest Xbox layoffs have reportedly hit id Software and Obsidian Entertainment especially hard. Reports indicate that id Software lost a very large portion of its staff, while Obsidian also saw deep cuts across development roles, raising serious questions about how both studios will maintain their existing project pipelines.

The cuts are especially worrying because of the studios involved. id Software remains one of the most important names in first-person shooters, while Obsidian is a key RPG developer inside Xbox\'s portfolio. Losing a large number of developers, testers, artists, producers, and support staff can disrupt more than one release window; it can also remove project knowledge that is difficult to rebuild once teams are reorganized.

The timing also clashes with Xbox\'s reported push to make first-party games a stronger reason to stay inside the ecosystem. If Microsoft wants more premium exclusives and healthier franchises, deep cuts at studios like id Software and Obsidian create an obvious tension between financial restructuring and long-term creative output.
