### Quick Report

Microsoft says it is actively migrating the remaining Control Panel functionality into the modern Settings app, with the long-term goal of retiring the classic Win32 control surface in Windows 11. Company design leadership described the process as deliberate because many networking and printer driver paths still depend on legacy-style control integrations.
<!-- more -->

The transition is part of a wider push toward WinUI-centric application models, but practical compatibility remains the key constraint before full deprecation can happen. For users and administrators, the immediate takeaway is gradual consolidation rather than a sudden removal, with legacy paths likely to persist until equivalent Settings coverage is proven stable.
