### Quick Report

Major PC vendors are reportedly securing DRAM allocations from Chinese memory maker CXMT as the market remains constrained by limited supply from SK hynix, Samsung, and Micron. According to reports, CXMT\'s capacity is already effectively booked through the end of 2027, leaving smaller OEMs with little room to source memory from the company.

The pressure is especially visible in the bargaining power of larger buyers. Dell, HP, and Apple are reportedly willing to sign larger contracts at higher prices, while smaller brands with less market leverage are being left behind. That dynamic underscores how memory supply can increasingly favor major ecosystem players over smaller manufacturers.
