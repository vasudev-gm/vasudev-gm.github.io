### Quick Report

AMD has expanded its Hawk Point lineup with 11 additional SKUs across the Ryzen 200 and Ryzen 100 series. The new models give laptop makers more ways to reuse the proven Zen 4-era mobile platform across mainstream systems, budget notebooks, and business-oriented designs.

<!-- more -->

This expansion is more about product coverage than a new architecture. Hawk Point remains a mature mobile platform, and that is useful for OEMs building laptops where cost, availability, battery life, and driver stability are more important than having the newest silicon. By spreading the lineup across Ryzen 200 and Ryzen 100 branding, AMD can keep serving mainstream notebooks while reserving its newer chips for more premium designs.

The additional SKUs should also give laptop vendors more flexibility when tuning power limits and price points. A thin-and-light productivity laptop, a budget student system, and a mainstream home notebook may all need slightly different CPU, GPU, and platform combinations, even if they are based on the same underlying family. Hawk Point gives AMD a familiar foundation to keep those segments covered.
