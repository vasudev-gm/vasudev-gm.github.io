### Quick Report

DeepComputing launched the DC-ROMA RISC-V Mainboard III for the Framework Laptop 13, bringing a modular RISC-V option to a familiar upgradeable notebook platform. The board starts at $699 and is based on the SpacemiT K3 RISC-V AI SoC.
<!-- more -->

The platform uses an 8-core CPU that can boost to 2.5 GHz and claims up to 60 TOPS of AI performance, while also supporting the RVA23 profile for better software consistency. It is meant to run mainstream Linux distributions such as Ubuntu and Fedora, positioning the board as a practical developer target rather than a pure experiment.
